Want Venmo on CalyxOS? Make this tweak to the Aurora Store.

üõç Some users won't be able to find common apps with the store's default settings. Here's how to get around that.

June 23, 2021

One of the benefits of CalyxOS is their integration of microG, a replacement for the Google Play Services. With it, you can use the Aurora Store, a mirror of the Google Play Store, to search for and download all of the same apps you'd typically use. Some users may not find some familiar apps like Intuit's Mint, or Paypal's Venmo, though. The reason is that the app works by serving content based on your Google account settings. The app's author registered his account in India, and anything that isn't available there won't be available to you wherever you are.

You have two options: Log in with your Google account (or another Google account that you have access to), or make a change in the network settings of the Aurora Store. We'll walk through the second option.

Step 1: Network Settings

Open the app and try searching for "Venmo." If you can't find it, go to Menu > Settings > Networking and toggle on "Insecure anonymous session." What this does is use the GSF ID, or Google Services Framework Identifier, that's generated from your phone when downloading from the Aurora Store. While this leaks a bit of information about your device, it still allows you to keep your Google account private. And if you use one of the built-in VPNs from The Calyx Institute or Riseup, you can keep your IP address hidden, too. Learn more about the GSF ID here.

Step 2: Authentication

Now that you have the GSM ID toggled on quit the app and restart it. Then, navigate to Menu > Accounts and log out, then log in again anonymously.

Tap the back button to get back to the main screen and search again for "Venmo." You should see it in your search results!
